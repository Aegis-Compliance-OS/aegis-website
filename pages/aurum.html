What Is AURUM

The defense supply chain cannot verify what it cannot measure. AURUM measures it.

Critical minerals — cobalt, nickel, titanium, lithium, rare earth elements — are the materials that define whether a defense system functions. The U.S. Department of Defense has designated a set of these minerals as strategically critical, and DFARS sourcing requirements increasingly demand documented provenance: not just a certificate of origin, but verifiable proof of where a mineral came from and what it is.

AURUM addresses this gap at the physical layer. By applying MXene-based electrochemical sensing — technology developed at the U.S. Army DEVCOM Chemical and Biological Center — AURUM generates voltammetric signatures from dissolved mineral samples that serve as physical-layer provenance fingerprints. Each mineral composition produces a distinct, measurable electrochemical signature.

Those signatures are structured through the STARS schema and output as DFARS-compliant chain-of-custody records, provenance attestation documents, and DoD sourcing compliance artifacts — from the extraction or processing point through the tier structure of the defense supply chain.

What Is a MXene?

MXenes are two-dimensional transition metal carbides and nitrides — a class of material with exceptionally high electrical conductivity and tunable surface chemistry. First synthesized in 2011, MXenes have demonstrated strong performance as electrochemical sensing substrates due to their high surface area, low electrical resistance, and capacity for selective functionalization.

The DEVCOM CBC patent (US 12,132,210) covers specific MXene-based electrocatalyst compositions and the methods for making and using them — including their application as selective electrochemical sensing surfaces for metal detection. DEVCOM CBC maintains its own MXene substrate synthesis capability, which supports the evaluation period under the patent license application. This makes the CBC license self-sufficient and independent of any separate synthesis patent.

Why MXene for Minerals

By tuning the metal composition on the MXene substrate to a specific critical mineral target, AURUM produces distinct, reproducible voltammetric signatures when exposed to dissolved mineral samples. These signatures are the physical-layer provenance fingerprint — measurable, repeatable, and directly linkable to source material characteristics.

How It Works

Electrochemical sensing converts mineral composition into verifiable identity.

AURUM's sensing process produces a physical-layer fingerprint — not a document, not a certificate — that travels with the mineral record through STARS into the compliance evidence chain.

Stage 01

Sample Preparation

A dissolved mineral sample is introduced to the AURUM sensing substrate. The MXene electrode surface — tuned to the target mineral's electrochemical profile — is exposed to the solution in a controlled measurement environment.

Stage 02

Voltammetric Measurement

An electrical potential is swept across the MXene electrode. The target mineral ions in solution undergo oxidation or reduction at the electrode surface, producing a characteristic current response — the voltammetric signature — at a potential specific to that mineral.

Stage 03

Signature Extraction

The peak potential, peak current, and peak shape of the voltammetric response are extracted and normalized. This data constitutes the physical-layer provenance fingerprint — distinct for each mineral composition and reproducible across measurements.

Stage 04

STARS Ingestion

The extracted signature data — mineral identity, concentration, peak parameters, measurement timestamp, and facility location — is structured through the STARS schema. The How Many table captures the quantitative measurement; the What, Where, and When tables anchor the provenance context.

Stage 05

Compliance Record Generation

The 8-Ball rule engine maps structured STARS output to the active regulatory catalog — DFARS sourcing requirements, DoD critical mineral attestation standards — and generates a framework-specific compliance artifact with cryptographic provenance.

STARS Integration

From electrochemical reading to DFARS compliance record.

Development Context

Why critical minerals. Why now.

The DoD's 2022 National Defense Industrial Strategy and subsequent DFARS rulemaking have placed critical minerals provenance verification on an accelerating compliance timeline. Prime contractors and Tier 1 suppliers are increasingly required to document not just where minerals were sourced, but to produce verifiable evidence of composition and origin.

Existing verification approaches — third-party certificates of origin, X-ray fluorescence spot testing, laboratory ICP-MS analysis — are expensive, slow, and produce documentation that exists outside any structured compliance record. They tell you what a mineral is; they do not produce the structured evidence a DFARS assessor requires.

AURUM is designed to fill that specific gap: physical-layer verification at the point of sampling, with STARS generating the structured evidence record in the same operational step.
