What Is PRISM

A remote examination cannot replace a physical one — unless it generates the same data.

Every musculoskeletal examination produces the same clinical outputs: joint angles, range of motion, force applied at each joint, time to complete each movement. In a clinical setting, those measurements are captured by a trained examiner in the room. In a telehealth setting, they disappear — replaced by a clinician's visual estimate and a patient's self-report.

PRISM closes that gap. Using a VA-developed virtual examination environment, patients perform structured movements against virtual objects while an RGB-D depth camera tracks skeletal pose in real time. An inverse dynamics solver estimates the actual force being exerted at each joint without on-body sensors. The result is a quantitative dataset clinically equivalent to an in-person assessment: objective, timestamped, and tied to a specific patient record.

STARS — embedded in PRISM — converts that dataset into structured clinical documentation automatically. The examination happens once. The HIPAA encounter record, the VA clinical documentation, and the telehealth compliance artifacts are generated in the same step.

The Problem PRISM Solves

✕Manual encounter documentation

VA clinicians using remote examination tools must separately document findings after the session — from memory or notes.

✕Separate HIPAA compliance workflow

Telehealth encounters require independent HIPAA documentation of PHI handling, access, and transmission — a parallel process to the clinical record.

✕Separate VA reporting requirements

VA clinical guidelines for musculoskeletal rehabilitation require structured outcome reporting that existing telehealth platforms do not generate automatically.

How It Works

From joint motion to clinical record — in the same session.

PRISM's sensing pipeline captures the physical data that remote examinations currently lose, structures it through STARS, and outputs the documentation that VA clinicians and compliance teams currently produce manually.

Stage 01

Virtual Examination

The patient performs a structured protocol — reaching, pushing, and lifting against virtual objects — designed to elicit specific joint movements across the elbow, wrist, and shoulder. A single RGB-D depth camera is the only hardware required on the patient side.

Stage 02

Skeletal Pose Tracking

The camera captures depth data in real time. PRISM tracks the patient's skeletal configuration throughout each movement — establishing joint position, orientation, and velocity continuously across the examination session.

Stage 03

Force Estimation

From the tracked skeletal data, PRISM estimates the actual force being exerted at each joint: angle in degrees, range of motion across the session, force in Newtons, time to complete each movement. Not a visual observation — a calculated physical quantity.

Stage 04

Compliance Output

The structured session dataset is processed through STARS and the 8-Ball rule engine, generating pre-structured documentation simultaneously across HIPAA, VA clinical guidelines, and FDA 21 CFR Part 11. One examination. All frameworks. No manual documentation step.
